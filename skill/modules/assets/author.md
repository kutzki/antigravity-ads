## Core Strategy: The Two-Lane Traffic System
Selling books via paid ads requires protecting the Amazon A10 algorithm. Traffic must be segmented based on intent.

### Lane A: Low-Intent Traffic (Social Media)
- **Platforms**: Meta (Facebook/Instagram), TikTok Ads.
- **Strategy**: Target broad interests, competitor authors, or celebrity hooks (e.g., the "Trojan Horse" strategy). 
- **Destination**: A **Bridge Page**. Never send cold social traffic directly to an Amazon listing. The bridge page presells the book and filters out non-buyers so your Amazon conversion rate stays high.
- **Tracking**: Install the Meta/TikTok Pixel on the Bridge Page. Use an **Amazon Attribution Link** on the "Buy on Amazon" button.

### Lane B: High-Intent Traffic (Search)
- **Platforms**: Amazon PPC (Sponsored Products/Brands), Google Search.
- **Strategy**: Target exact match keywords indicating high purchase intent (e.g., "dating advice book for men").
- **Destination**: Direct to the Amazon Product Page (via Attribution link if using Google).

## Amazon PPC Architecture
1. **Discovery**: Auto-targeting campaigns (low budget) to harvest long-tail keywords.
2. **Scaling**: Manual Exact match campaigns on high-converting keywords with Top-of-Search bid modifiers.
3. **Defensive**: Sponsored Product ads targeting your *own* ASINs to prevent competitors from advertising on your book's page.

## Creative Strategy
- **Meta/TikTok**: High-contrast, scroll-stopping visuals. Focus on the core problem/pain point the book solves (using the PAS or BAB framework). Avoid "buy my book" graphics.
- **Amazon A+ Content**: Build out visual A+ Content modules on the listing (author story, inside look, comparison charts) to maximize the conversion rate of paid traffic.

## KDP Listing Optimization
Before scaling ad spend, ensure:
1. Title exactly matches the cover.
2. 7 backend keyword boxes are strictly under 50 characters each (do not use "book" or celebrity names).
3. The description is fully formatted using HTML (`<h4>` to `<h6>`, `<b>`, `<ul>`).
